Audio Samples for Variational Bayesian IVA

by Natalie Hanekom and Johan Hanekom

Description

This page presents selected audio samples for the results presented in the Master's dissertation "Bayesian Source of Interest Extraction", which performs source of interest extraction by using speaker-dependent Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) in a probabilistic source separation algorithm. The algorithm, called variational Bayesian independent vector analysis (VB IVA), was evaluated on three datasets (A, B and C) and compared against piloted OverIVA, an EM IVA algorithm with GMM source models and the SpeakerBeam neural network. Audio samples are provided to compare the source extraction quality of the different algorithms for the three datasets. Datasets A and B were created in MATLAB using speech signals from LibriVox (www.librivox.com) recordings. Dataset A contains two female sources of interest with 10 background speakers, forming diffuse babble noise. Dataset B contains three male sources of interest with conversational speech and 10 background speakers, forming diffuse babble noise. Conversational speech contains many silences. The sources in dataset C were recorded with a microphone array in a conference room. The sources of interest are a mother and daughter with conversational speech. There are also four background speakers. The VB IVA and OverIVA audio samples presented here for Datasets A and B are for a circular 8-microphone array with 8 cm spacing, and a 2-microphone array with 8 cm spacing for the EM IVA algorithm, which is a determined 2x2 algorithm. SpeakerBeam is a single-channel neural network algorithm. The reverberation time is 200 ms. Dataset C was recorded with a linear 8-microphone array with 5 cm spacing. The SNR was set as the ratio of the total energy of the sources of interest and the total energy of the interfering sources. Audio samples are provided for 10 dB and 5 dB SNR. Audio samples are provided for the maximum performance of all the algorithms. For more details on the algorithms, their settings, as well as the datasets, please refer to the relevant dissertation.
